When to use Listy

Use Listy when you want to publish a full-page piece in which most of the content is clip-like: that is, it breaks down naturally into sections. Listy is not currently set up to make embedded lists; we will be exploring this possibility more in the near future.

The introductory text of your list (the "leadin" field) should be brief. Don't start with a 500 word preamble; the list items should make up the main substance of the article.

Ideally, each section of your list (or list item) should contain more than headline and text; if it does not, consider using an article with subheads.

Listy is designed to work best with a strong visuals complementing each list item. If your piece does not lend itself to using visuals, consider whether a traditional article may be a better template.

Spend some time looking at examples of lists we have published so far. Digital News Design has also created a document with more tips for creating successful lists. If you still aren't sure how to proceed, please contact newstools@nytimes.com for guidance.

Instructions

  1. Find existing lists on the homepage of Listy. You can filter the list by typing in the search field, or sort using the links on the upper right.

    Create a list by clicking the green "New list" button, and completing the form in the modal that opens. You can go back and edit any of these fields later by clicking the "Edit List Info" button at the upper left of the list edit page.

  2. On the list edit page, add one or more items by clicking the green "Add Items" button. Click the headline or "Edit" button of each item to edit that item. The "Expand Items" button to the upper right will open all items at once for editing.

  3. Your Listy will automatically insert an ad placeholder after the fourth list item, and every third list item thereafter.

    These can be re-ordered and deleted. To insert an ad after the current item, use the "Add Advertisement" button at the bottom of the screen.

  4. You can preview your list in a new tab at any time by clicking the "Preview" button in the upper right. This will not create or update a Scoop asset. Please note, you cannot refresh the preview page to see subsequent changes; you must generate a new preview by clicking the "Preview" button again.
  5. To export your list to Scoop, click the "Create in Scoop" button in the upper right. Select a section, provide a slug and press "Save to Scoop". You will be taken directly to your Scoop asset. In Scoop, remember to add a summary, byline, and promotional image.
  6. Click "Update Scoop" to save subsequent changes to your Scoop asset.
  7. Note that creating or updating a Scoop asset will never publish it. You must still publish your Listy manually in Scoop.
  8. Will your list be appearing in print? If so, please follow the instructions for making it available to Today's Paper and Kindle. (And note the caveats for lists heavy on the visuals.)
